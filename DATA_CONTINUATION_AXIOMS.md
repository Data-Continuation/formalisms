# Data Continuation Axioms

## Axiom 0 — Reality-binding is not a property of data

Reality-binding is not inherent in data. It is a governed continuation state.

## Axiom 1 — Data is representational until it seeks continuation

A datum may exist, be stored, generated, displayed, predicted, or replayed without becoming consequence-binding.

## Axiom 2 — Continuation is not existence

Data can exist without being permitted to continue into reality.

## Axiom 3 — Prediction is not continuation authority

A predicted future state has no right to become real merely because it is plausible.

## Axiom 4 — Plausibility is not admissibility

A transition being possible does not imply that it may bind.

## Axiom 5 — Continuation requires a declared role

A datum may not continue into consequence until the role it is being asked to occupy is declared.

## Axiom 6 — Continuation requires a transition class

A datum may not continue into consequence until the type of crossing is identified.

## Axiom 7 — Transition classes require governance blocks

Each class of continuation has required authority, evidence, trust, risk, reversibility, and failure constraints.

## Axiom 8 — Data must not change role without admissibility

A datum admissible as representation is not automatically admissible as evidence, authority, command, or consequence.

## Axiom 9 — Admissibility is evaluated at commit

Prior approval, policy history, or replayability cannot substitute for current admissibility at the boundary.

## Axiom 10 — Composite continuation can exceed local continuation

Individually admissible data transitions may compose into an inadmissible consequence.

## Axiom 11 — Continuity decays

Authority, evidence, state freshness, trust, and reversibility degrade unless re-established.

## Axiom 12 — No basis means no continuation

If admissibility cannot be established, data must not become reality-binding.

## Axiom 13 — Denial is a governed event

A denied or fail-closed continuation must be preserved as evidence.

## Axiom 14 — Admitted continuation must be replayable

If data becomes consequence, the basis for that crossing must be reconstructable.

## Axiom 15 — Replay is not reversal

A consequence may be reconstructable without being recoverable.

## Axiom 16 — Local admissibility does not imply composite admissibility

For transitions T₁...Tₙ:

```text
∀i Admit(Tᵢ, x) = true
does not imply
Admit(T₁ ∘ ... ∘ Tₙ, x) = true
```

## Axiom 17 — The inference window is non-binding

The inference window permits recursive classification, testing, repair, and denial before data becomes consequence-binding.

## Axiom 18 — Inference-window collapse requires fail-closed behavior

If the inference window collapses before admissibility is established, the continuation must fail closed.

## Axiom 19 — Governance is the law of data continuation

Governance determines whether data may continue from representation into consequence.
